I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Turin since November 2024. My research field is theoretical and computational Astroparticle Physics. The aim of my research is to understand the origin and propagation of cosmic rays. My work includes the modelling of cosmic-ray physics to understand the cosmic-ray spectra measured at Earth and the contributions from different sources to them, such as pulsars. I am also interested in understanding the nature of dark matter, for which I work on indirect detection strategies such as cosmic rays. I also study the effect of dark matter on celestial bodies, especially on the evolution and energetics of stars.

Before coming to Turin, I pursued a PhD in Theoretical and Computational Astroparticle Physics at Stockholm University and The Oskar Klein Centre with Tim Linden. In September 2024, I defended my PhD thesis "How The Positron Became Cool: A Study of Cosmic-Ray Positrons From Pulsars and Dark Matter" .
